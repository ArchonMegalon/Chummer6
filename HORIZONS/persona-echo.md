# Persona Echo

![Persona Echo banner](../assets/horizons/persona-echo.png "continuity without letting the software become your runner's lying publicist.")<br>_[continuity without letting the software become your runner's lying publicist.](../assets/horizons/persona-echo.png)_

**Character history with a paper trail, not a PR department.**

_Status: Horizon only — future idea, not active build work._

## What problem does this solve?

Most digital sheets treat your runner like a fresh install every session, scrubbing the context of that glitchy cyberarm or the specific street-doc who stitched your fragged kidney. If there is 'memory,' it usually feels like a cheap hallucination—sanitizing the specific scars and ge-tags until your legend is just flat data. You want a dossier that remembers the heat, not just the current numbers.

## A real table scene

GM: "The Fixer asks if you're the same crew that burned the Renraku subsidiary in '24." STREET SAM: "Check the Echo. My internal clock says I was in a vat in Vladivostok that month." GM: "He cross-references the signature. The receipt matches your old OS version. He's impressed." DECKER: "Good thing we didn't let the software 'optimize' that log entry away." STREET SAM: "Scars stay. Data stays. No lies." GM: "He slides a credstick across the table. 'Honesty is rare in this sprawl. Keep it that way.'"

## Meanwhile, Chummer is doing this

- Tightening the math engine so every stat change comes with a timestamped 'why.' - Ensuring your history survives the static even when you're running offline. - Polishing the dossier view to clearly mark the difference between cold fact and runner legend.

## Why that would be great

It turns your character sheet into a living witness. Instead of a sterile record update, Persona Echo tracks the evolution of your runner with receipts. Every piece of gear, every contact, and every trauma carries its origin forward. It’s the difference between a high-gloss recruitment brochure and a grimy, honest dossier that knows exactly where you were when the sky turned green.

## Why it is still a Horizon

Right now, most code-monkeys are obsessed with 'stateless' sheets—which is just dev-speak for 'I'm too lazy to manage your history.' We're gutting the amateur habit of treating your runner's life like a disposable buffer. We need the logic to be bulletproof so your history doesn't fragment the second your grid connection goes ghost. Until we can guarantee the receipts won't burn, it stays on the horizon.

## What would need to exist first

- grounded evidence
- approval states
- clean hub and media ownership
