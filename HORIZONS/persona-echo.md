**Memory with receipts. Identity without the hallucinated hype.**

_Status: Horizon only — future idea, not active build work._

## What problem does this solve?

Most software treats character progression like a sterile database update, scrubbing the specific scars and gear-pedigree that actually make a runner real. If your tool can’t tell the difference between a shop-bought AK-97 and the one you pried from a Red Samurai’s cold fingers, it’s not a dossier—it’s a grocery list. Digital sheets shouldn't just track your stats; they should remember the price you paid for them.

## Why that would be great

Persona Echo transforms your character from a disposable file into a living dossier. It carries forward 'Approved Facts'—gear history, contact grudges, and specific trauma—without letting the software 'hallucinate' a legend you didn't earn. It keeps inference honestly labeled, ensuring your runner’s history feels like a life lived, not just a pile of accumulated XP. It’s about building a digital witness that respects the weight of your story.

## Why it is still a Horizon

We’re busy building a witness, not just a calculator. Creating a local-first provenance engine that handles layered history without corrupting your save file is a surgical process. We’d rather deliver a bulletproof vault of memories later than a leaky bucket of 'maybe-stats' that forgets your runner's name the moment the Grid goes dark. Accuracy is a slow-cook process in a world of fast-food software.

## What would need to exist first

- grounded evidence
- approval states
- clean hub and media ownership
