**Continuity without the PR department.**

_Status: Horizon only — future idea, not active build work._

## What problem does this solve?

Most digital sheets treat your runner like a fresh install every session, scrubbing the context of that glitchy cyber-arm or that suspicious connection in the Matrix. When history gets laundered into a flat stat block, you lose the provenance—the actual evidence of where your karma went and why that fixer still owes you a favor. It’s the difference between a character who has lived through the drek and one who was just generated by a lazy algorithm.

## Meanwhile, Chummer is doing this

- Hardening the rule-scripting tools so your personal history doesn't conflict with the global SR4 core.

## Why that would be great

It stops your software from being a lying publicist. Persona Echo treats your character as a persistent dossier, carrying approved facts forward while labeling inferences honestly. It’s about grounded evidence—keeping the cool parts of your legend tied to actual table moments instead of just XP sinks. When your sheet shows exactly where a modifier came from, it’s not just a number; it’s a story with a timestamp and a witness.

## Why it is still a Horizon

Building a system that remembers your sins without bloating your local storage into a nightmare requires a level of data-integrity we’re still hardening. We’re busy making sure the basic SR4 rules don't catch fire before we start adding the 'memory' layer. If we rushed this, your runner’s history would be about as reliable as a used deck from a Redmond pawn shop. We want your legend to be bulletproof, not a collection of glitchy ghosts.

## What would need to exist first

- grounded evidence
- approval states
- clean hub and media ownership
