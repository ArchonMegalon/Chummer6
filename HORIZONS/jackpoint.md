# JACKPOINT

![JACKPOINT banner](../assets/horizons/jackpoint.png "coffee stains optional, evidence boundaries not.")<br>_[coffee stains optional, evidence boundaries not.](../assets/horizons/jackpoint.png)_

**Turn street-level noise into evidence heavy enough to break a corporate desk.**

_Status: Horizon only — future idea, not active build work._

## What problem does this solve?

Most digital dossier tools are just glorified fan-fiction bots. They’ll invent a contact's loyalty rating or a firewall's encryption level with the unearned confidence of a corp-strat AI. When the lead flies and the GM asks for a source, 'it looked cool on the screen' is a fast way to get burned. Narrative output is a liability if you can't trace the data back to a hard receipt.

## A real table scene

GM: 'The Renraku analyst stares at your data burst. He's looking for a hole.'

## Meanwhile, Chummer is doing this

- Hardening the evidence-receipt engine to track data pedigree across sessions.
- Refining the source-classification tags so 'Street Scuttlebutt' never gets confused with 'Ares Internal.'
- Mapping the media-factory hooks to ensure every dossier entry has a visual receipt to match.

## Why that would be great

Jackpoint is for the runner who values the truth as much as the pay. It takes your raw session logs, loot tables, and contact interactions and filters them through a grounded evidence sieve. You get professional-grade briefings where every stat, modifier, and gear claim comes with a digital paper trail. It’s not just a pretty layout; it’s a verified history that can survive a corporate audit or a hostile GM’s 'prove it' stare.

## Why it is still a Horizon

We’re not shipping a creative writing assistant. Building an engine that can distinguish between a hard data-tap and a drunken rumor requires a rock-solid registry and a media pipeline that doesn't just lose files in a folder soup. We’re waiting until the long-range plan for data-integrity is fully decrypted before we let Jackpoint start stamping your files.

## What would need to exist first

- grounded evidence receipts
- approval states
- registry/media seam clarity
- source classification
- bounded publication workflows

## Pitch your own future

If you’ve got a better way to prove a data-trail is legit without checking a thousand spreadsheets, pitch it on the tracker.
