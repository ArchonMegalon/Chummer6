## What problem does this solve?

The 'Homebrew Paradox' hits every table eventually: you start with a simple 'common sense' tweak and end up with a spreadsheet that requires a degree in forensic accounting to explain. Most house rules are born from developers who think 'I'll just change this one line' is a valid architectural strategy, turning your character sheet into a haunted graveyard of unexplainable damage calculations and forked-code folklore.

## Why that would be great

Karma Forge replaces 'trust me' folklore with surgical rule-layering. Instead of hacking the engine and praying it doesn't crash mid-session, you slot in a controlled overlay stack that can be inspected, previewed, and rolled back. Every modifier comes with an 'explain receipt'—a transparent path showing exactly where that +1 came from and what other rules it’s talking to. It’s professional-grade transparency for GMs who are tired of being the group’s volunteer debugger.

## Why it is still a Horizon

This is a long-range plan currently under decryption. While the core engine is being hardened for standard runs, the Forge remains a high-concept horizon project. We're prioritizing a rock-solid foundation for existing eras before we give everyone the keys to rewrite physics. It's the difference between a stable cyber-deck and one currently being rewired in a basement with a soldering iron and a dream.

## What would need to exist first
