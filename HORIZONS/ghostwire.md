## What problem does this solve?

After a four-hour crawl through a Renraku black-site, every runner becomes a professional liar. The Decker swears the silent alarm was a glitch, the Rigger claims they were in 'passive mode,' and the Street Sam is certain they didn't pull the trigger first. Rules disputes aren't just annoying; they are a memory contest with worse lighting that kills the session's momentum faster than a decker hitting a black IC wall.

## Why that would be great

Ghostwire treats your session like a crime scene, providing a forensic reconstruction of every state change and trigger-pull. Instead of shouting over the pizza boxes about whether a modifier was active, you get replayable receipts that show the objective truth of the table. It’s a black box for your campaign’s worst disasters, ensuring that when a decker claims the 'implied state' of the grid was in their favor, the logs can politely disagree.

## Why it is still a Horizon

This is a data-haven currently under heavy decryption. We’re busy hardening the core engine and the SR4 rulesets first, because a forensic replay is only as good as the math it’s recording. We aren't shipping a 'maybe' engine; we're building the deterministic foundation required to make session history actually mean something at the table. Once the long-range plan hits the next milestone, we’ll start scrubbing the echoes.

## What would need to exist first

- session authority and event history
- evidence labeling
- replayable receipts
- clean sync seams
