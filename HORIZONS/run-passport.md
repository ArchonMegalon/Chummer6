# RUN PASSPORT

![RUN PASSPORT banner](../assets/horizons/run-passport.png "crossing rule borders without smuggling cursed assumptions in your coat.")<br>_[crossing rule borders without smuggling cursed assumptions in your coat.](../assets/horizons/run-passport.png)_

**Carry your runner's digital DNA across any grid without the math turning into eldritch contraband.**

_Status: Horizon only — future idea, not active build work._

## What problem does this solve?

Portability is a cheap promise until your SR4 rigger hits a house-ruled host and the compatibility matrix starts screaming. Most 'import' buttons are just funeral rites for your stats because developers usually prioritize 'making it look pretty' over 'making the math survive a ruleset collision'.

## Meanwhile, Chummer is doing this

- The core Lua engine is learning how to explain its math receipts without a manual. - SR4 plugin stability is being hammered into a shape that won't brick your mobile deck. - Mobile session shells are getting the tactical HUD polish needed for actual table heat.

## Why that would be great

Run Passport turns your character into a verifiable runtime identity instead of a pile of raw hope. It bundles your runtime stack profile and lineage into a 'digital DNA' packet that any Chummer6-compliant node can parse, providing compatibility projections that tell you exactly how 'cursed' your assumptions are before you ever roll a die on a new grid.

## Why it is still a Horizon

Building a universal translator for rulesets written by different authors across three decades is a special kind of data-hell. We are still refining the logic gates to ensure that when you 'cross the border,' your character doesn't arrive as a collection of null-pointers and broken dreams. We'd rather keep this concept in the shadows than ship a converter that deletes your favorite cyberdeck because of a rounding error.

## What would need to exist first

- runtime stack profile
- fingerprint and lineage
- compatibility projections
