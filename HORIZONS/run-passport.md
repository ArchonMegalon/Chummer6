## What problem does this solve?

The problem is 'data rot' and lazy math. Portability is a cheap promise made by devs who haven't seen a real character sheet since 2015. You spend weeks perfecting a runner, only for them to become 'eldritch contraband' the moment you switch hosts because someone's hard-coded assumptions didn't account for your specific edge-case cyberware. Most sheets are just fragile collections of hope that turn into corrupted noise the moment they leave their home grid.

## Meanwhile, Chummer is doing this

- Hardening the core Lua engine to ensure deterministic math that survives a cross-grid transit. - Scripting the SR4 plugin contracts so your legacy runner doesn't brick the moment it hits a mobile shell. - Refacing the provenance receipts to prove exactly which rulebook authorized that questionable attribute boost.

## Why that would be great

It turns your character into a verified digital identity that GMs can actually trust. Instead of spending two hours 'auditing' a new player's sheet to find the broken math, the Passport provides a high-contrast compatibility receipt. It’s the difference between a handwritten scrap of paper and a cryptographic proof that your runner’s DNA is rule-legal, deterministic, and ready for the table.

## Why it is still a Horizon

Because building a universal translator for Shadowrun math is like trying to defuse a data-bomb with rusty cyber-spurs. We’re currently roasting the lazy code habits that allow 'it worked on my machine' math drift. This stays on the horizon as part of the long-range plan until the migration previews are 100% deterministic, ensuring you don't lose half your essence just because a host-swap went sideways.
