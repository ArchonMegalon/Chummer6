# RUN PASSPORT

![RUN PASSPORT banner](../assets/horizons/run-passport.png "customs for rule eras, with less bribery and more receipts.")

**One runner, multiple rule eras, zero blind jumps.**

_Status: Horizon only - future idea, not active build work._

_Image idea: a runner's sheet going through a cyberpunk customs gate while one rules era stamps "cleared" and another glares at one illegal gear interaction._

## What problem does this solve?

Cross-era portability sounds great right up until a transfer mutates stats in the dark. RUN PASSPORT is the idea of making migration honest: what survives, what bends, what breaks, and why.

## A real table scene

> One runner is standing at the border between rule eras with paperwork in hand.
>
> The player wants to bring a beloved runner into a new campaign and would prefer customs not confiscate their personality.
> **Player:** "I want to bring this runner into the new campaign."
> **GM:** "Fine, but I do not want a secret stat mutation halfway through chargen."
> **Chummer6:** "Migration preview ready. 82 percent carried cleanly. Two qualities remapped. One gear interaction blocked."
> **Player:** "Can I see what changed before I commit?"
> **Chummer6:** "Yes. Nothing crosses the border without a receipt."
> **GM:** "Now that is a customs officer I can live with."

## Meanwhile, Chummer is doing this

- fingerprinting the runtime identity of the character
- comparing source and destination rule environments
- surfacing preserved, changed, and blocked elements separately
- logging provenance so the jump is inspectable instead of magical

## Why that would be great

Tables could move characters between rule environments without blind faith, post-migration regret, or the usual export/import theater.

## Why it is still a Horizon

Honest migration needs runtime stack modeling, lineage, and compatibility projections that are mature enough to survive real campaign pressure.

## What would need to exist first

- runtime stack profile
- fingerprint and lineage
- compatibility projections
