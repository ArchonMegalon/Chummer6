**Your runner's digital DNA, verified across any grid.**

_Status: Horizon only — future idea, not active build work._

## What problem does this solve?

Portability is easy to promise but painful to implement once the compatibility matrices actually start looking at your 'unique' gear logic.

## A real table scene

The character crossed a rules border and customs is in a bad mood.

> **Player**<br>
> "Can I move this runner into the new environment?"

> **GM**<br>
> "Depends whether the gear is legal or eldritch."

> **Chummer6**<br>
> "Migration preview ready. Two loadout items fail. One quality mutates. One habit survives because the dev forgot to kill it."

> **Player**<br>
> "So this is a passport and a threat assessment."

## Meanwhile, Chummer is doing this

- carrying runtime identity and lineage with the character
- projecting compatibility before the jump happens
- flagging illegal or drifting interactions early
- making migration a previewable process instead of a leap of faith

## Why that would be great

Portability gets honest: less surprise breakage, less hidden drift, and fewer characters smuggling cursed assumptions across borders.

## Why it is still a Horizon

The registry seam and runtime stack model are still hardening in the forge; we're not shipping a passport that gets you shot by a border-drone.

## What would need to exist first

- runtime stack profile
- fingerprint and lineage
- compatibility projections
