## What problem does this solve?

Devs love to call a catastrophic database mutation a 'tiny admin tweak' until the UI starts bleeding neon. The moment nobody can answer who approved a change, every serious operator action starts smelling like haunted button mashing. Without a casket, your 'quick fix' becomes a permanent ghost in the machine that nobody knows how to exorcise.

## Why that would be great

Command Casket turns every high-stakes operator move into an auditable capsule. It binds the requester, the approval state, and the outcome into a single, permanent record. Most importantly, it attaches a heavy-duty rollback handle to every change. If a tweak starts smoking, you bury it immediately without digging through cryptic logs or praying to the ghosts in the machine.

## Why it is still a Horizon

This is a heavy-duty stabilizer for a system that is still finding its legs. We are currently focused on making the core SR4 rules and Lua scripting engine bulletproof. Once the engine is deterministic and reliable, we will build the Casket to record the legends—and the mistakes—that happen on top of it.

## What would need to exist first

- approval-aware workflows
- preview, apply, and rollback receipts
- auditable command capsules
